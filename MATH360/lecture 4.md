given
$$
\frac{d^{2}\mathbf{x}}{dt^{2}}. = - \frac{\mathbf{x}}{||\mathbf{x}||^3}
$$

looks like
$$
\begin{align}
x_{1}'' &  = -\frac{x_{1}}{||\mathbf{x}||^3} \\
x_{2}'' & = \frac{x_{2}}{||\mathbf{x}||^3}
\end{align}
$$
set $u_{0}=\mathbf{x}$

then 
$$
\implies \mathbf{u}' = A\mathbf{u}, \quad  \mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix}
\mathbf{x}  \\
\mathbf{y}
\end{bmatrix}
$$


ANS:
$$
\begin{align}
||\mathbf{x}||^3  & = ||\sqrt{ (x_{1}^{2}+x_{2}^{2}) }||^{3}  \\
 & = (x_{1}^{2}+x_{2}^{2})^{3/2}
\end{align}
$$
